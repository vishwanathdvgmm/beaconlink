# 1. Purpose

This document defines the intended users of BeaconLink.

Understanding the target audience ensures that product decisions remain
focused and aligned with the project's mission.

BeaconLink is not designed to solve every hosting problem.

It is designed to solve the right problems for the right users.

---

# 2. User Classification

BeaconLink categorizes users into four groups.

- Primary Users
- Secondary Users
- Future Users
- Non-Target Users

This classification helps prioritize product development.

---

# 3. Primary Users

These users represent the primary audience for BeaconLink.

## Students

Students frequently build projects for:

- coursework
- hackathons
- research
- portfolios

BeaconLink enables them to deploy projects without paying recurring hosting
costs while learning modern deployment practices.

---

## Independent Developers

Developers who build:

- personal websites
- APIs
- experimental applications
- open-source projects

can self-host with minimal infrastructure knowledge.

---

## Freelancers

Freelancers often need to host:

- portfolios
- client demonstrations
- prototypes
- temporary deployments

BeaconLink provides a professional deployment experience while preserving
ownership.

---

## Hobbyists

Technology enthusiasts and home-lab users frequently enjoy operating
their own infrastructure.

BeaconLink simplifies deployment while allowing them to retain complete
control.

---

## Open Source Contributors

Developers maintaining personal or community projects can deploy
applications without relying entirely on commercial hosting providers.

---

# 4. Secondary Users

These users benefit from BeaconLink but are not the primary design focus.

## Small Businesses

Businesses requiring:

- company websites
- internal tools
- lightweight applications

can reduce infrastructure costs while maintaining ownership.

---

## Educational Institutions

Schools and universities can host educational resources using existing
hardware.

---

## Non-Profit Organizations

Organizations with limited budgets can self-host applications using
hardware they already own.

---

## Research Groups

Research teams often require temporary application deployments.

BeaconLink simplifies deployment without requiring dedicated cloud
infrastructure.

---

# 5. Future Users

Future versions of BeaconLink may expand support for additional users.

Potential audiences include:

- enterprise teams
- managed service providers
- hosting consultants
- government organizations
- distributed edge deployments

These users are not currently part of BeaconLink's primary product strategy.

---

# 6. Users Outside the Target Audience

BeaconLink is currently **not intended** for users requiring:

- hyperscale cloud infrastructure
- global content delivery networks
- large-scale Kubernetes orchestration
- managed virtual machine hosting
- enterprise cloud migration platforms
- public Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS)

These problems are better addressed by specialized platforms.

---

# 7. User Personas

## Persona A — Student

Needs:

- Portfolio hosting
- Low cost
- Easy deployment
- HTTPS
- Custom domain

---

## Persona B — Independent Developer

Needs:

- Reliable deployments
- Multiple applications
- Logs
- Monitoring
- Secure remote access

---

## Persona C — Home Lab Enthusiast

Needs:

- Full infrastructure ownership
- Advanced configuration
- Plugin support
- Multiple devices

---

## Persona D — Small Business Owner

Needs:

- Company website
- Simple management
- High reliability
- Minimal maintenance

---

# 8. User Needs

Regardless of experience level, BeaconLink users generally require:

- Ownership
- Security
- Simplicity
- Reliability
- Transparency
- Cross-platform compatibility
- Easy deployment
- Custom domains
- Automatic HTTPS
- Health monitoring

BeaconLink should prioritize these needs throughout the product lifecycle.

---

# 9. Summary

BeaconLink is designed primarily for individuals and organizations who wish
to self-host applications without becoming infrastructure experts.

The platform should continue evolving to serve these users while
remaining focused on its core mission.

Future expansion into additional markets should occur only when it does
not compromise BeaconLink's product principles.

> **BeaconLink is built for people who want to own their infrastructure—not
> rent someone else's.**
